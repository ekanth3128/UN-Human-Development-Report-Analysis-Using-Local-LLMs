On the other hand, the forecast decreasing pattern in the dependen-
cy ratios at the national level after 2050 does not apply to all lower
spatial levels. Out of 25 districts in Serbia, 9 would experience an in-
crease in the ageing index, 12 in the share of the older population, 13
in old age dependency ratio and 14 in total dependency ratio through-
out the whole century . The majority of districts with increasing age-
ing indicators refer to those with currently older population than in
other districts and with prominent out-migration. When the whole
projection period is considered, there would be only 7 districts with
the ageing index lower than today by the end of this century and just
one in case of old age dependency ratio. Yet, the increase of the in-
dex would be lower than 15% in 7 districts, and higher than one third
in 14 districts, of which 4 districts would experience double the cur-
rent index value by 2100. Finally, 2 of 25 districts would have high-
er number of the older population than the working age population
already by 2050, and 8 districts by 2100, all of which located in the
two south regions (Table 6). This suggests that sustainability of that
area would be seriously endangered, which would also contribute to
further deepening of the already big gap in terms of the development
level between districts.

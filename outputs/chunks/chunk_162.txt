Therefore, cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of death in
Serbia. Over the last decade, the mortality rates from cardiovascular
diseases fell from 774.2 per 100.000 to 753.4 per 100.000 residents
(Chart 6).
In 2019, 52.330 people in the Republic of Serbia died from cardiovas-
cular diseases. Females were dying more often than males. While the
mortality rate in females was 792.3 per 100,000, in males it was 712.6
per 100,000. This female/male mortality ratio is completely oppo-
site to the EU ratio where the rate for men is 1.4 times higher than
for women (1.1 times higher in Serbia) (Eurostat, 2021b). Although
in Serbia women use health services and visit specialists more often

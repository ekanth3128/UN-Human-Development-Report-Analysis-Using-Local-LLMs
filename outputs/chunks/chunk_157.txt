Grozdanov et al., 2014).
In Serbia, in 2019, 86.2% of the population used bread in their diet
every day. The use of animal fats for meal preparation increased in
2019 (39.2%) compared to 2013 and 2006 (25.9% and 33.6%, respec-
tively) (Milić et al., 2021).
Based on this data, we can conclude that people in Serbia have been
worsening their eating habits in recent years, especially in the intake
of dairy products and fruit and vegetable consumption. The reasons
are numerous and range from a faster pace of life, modernization, to
availability and affordability of unhealthy. For these reasons, addition-
al efforts are needed to raise awareness among the population of the
importance of healthy eating, and of adherence to eating recommen-
dations, which would significantly reduce the risk of chronic disease.

9% of the population cycling for at least 10 minutes per day. This sort
of activity is somewhat more present in Voivodina (23.5%) compared to
other geographical areas, probably due to the flat terrain in Voivodina
and existing cycling paths (Milić et al., 2021; Grozdanov et al., 2014).

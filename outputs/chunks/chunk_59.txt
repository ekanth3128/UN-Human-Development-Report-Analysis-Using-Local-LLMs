1.7  Human capital – strong
subnational differences
A demographic profile of any modern society has been long since be-
yond the simple narrative of the population size and its sex and age
structure. However, policy makers and general public in countries
facing depopulation and long periods of sub-replacement fertility,
as is the case with Serbia, are still more focused on raw population
numbers and crude demographic rates despite increasing research ev-
idence suggesting that specific characteristics of population, such as
educational attainment, skills, good health, and financial wellbeing,
might play a decisive role in shaping demographic patterns in the long
run. This is where the concept of human capital could help in better
understanding the complex nature of the current demographic chal-
lenges in Serbia, especially when it comes to subnational differentials.

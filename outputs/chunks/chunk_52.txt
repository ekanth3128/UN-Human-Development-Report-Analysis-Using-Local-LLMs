Table 1 illustrates the continuous population ageing of Serbia be-
tween the census year of 1991 (coincided with the dissolution of the
former Yugoslavia) and 2019, with the share of the older population
that surpassed the share of the young in 2019. Expectedly, female
population is older than male population due to the higher life expec-
tancy particularly at older ages, which is typical for developed world
regions (Devedžić & Stojilković, 2012).
Of the four regions in Serbia, the region of South and East Serbia has
the highest median age of 44.1 years, while its oldest district (Zaječar)
reached 47.8 years in 2019 (SORS, 2021a). It has resulted from the two
factors – this region has experienced the country’s biggest plunge in
total fertility rate, with some of its districts were being particularly
hit by the the first post-war wave of emigration of ‘guest workers’ to
the West Europe between the late 1960s and 1980s. This early emi-
gration wave consisting of of baby-boomers once more impacts the
current age structure of population in traditional emigration areas
through its retired returnees. Generally, those areas are typically ru-
ral and less developed compared to others in the country (Penev &
Predojević-Despić, 2012; Nikitović et al., 2015).
1.3  Birth crisis
The birth crisis in Serbia is reflected in the decades-long sub-replace-
ment fertility which is induced by the postponement of the first birth
until increasingly older age, the low share of higher birth orders, the
increasing share of childless women across all reproductive ages in-
cluding permanent childlessness, and the very high total induced
abortion rate.
The period of below-replacement fertility in Serbia began in the late
1950s after a much shorter post-war baby boom than in most European
countries in spite of the same common drivers of fertility decline, such
as the adoption of new norms and values and growing shares of the fe-
male labour force (Kupiszewski et al., 2012). The TFR was lower by 15
per cent than the replacement level already by 1971. The interaction of

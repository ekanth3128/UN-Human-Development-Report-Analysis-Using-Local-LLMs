As presented in the figure 4, increase in annual temperature (left) and
summer temperature (centre) is expected, together with a decrease
of summer precipitation in most regions in the country (right).219 Vul-
nerable areas in Serbia cover around 57% percent of territory which
is prone to heatwaves, drought, floods, landslide hazards, forest fires
and erosion (Dragicevic et al., 2011). Greater increase in average tem-
perature is expected in southern Serbia in comparison to the north-
ern parts of the country.220 Except changes in mean annual and mean
seasonal values of the essential climate variables, temperature and
precipitation, changes of the different extremes are also projected
(Vukovic et al., 2018; Djurdjevic et al., 2018). The number of hot and
tropical days will continue to increase, and heat waves will become
more intense and more frequent in the future. Extreme heat waves,
which were rare during the reference period, will occur on average
at least 2-3 times a year by the middle of the twenty-first century. The
changes in precipitation extremes indicate a further intensification of
the already observed changes.221

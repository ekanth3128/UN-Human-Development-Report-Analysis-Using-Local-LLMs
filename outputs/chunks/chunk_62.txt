Depopulation in terms of the shrinking and ageing population of Ser-
bia is commonly interpreted at the national level with no or little ef-
fort to be appropriately considered from the sub-national perspective.
However, depopulation is a much more regional and sub-regional demo-
graphic challenge than a national-level issue. Yet, it is often overlooked
or incorrectly addressed in relevant strategic and policy papers. The
aim of this part of the chapter is to underline the importance of tai-
loring policies tackling depopulation challenge to subnational demo-
graphic specificities by means of interpreting the projections of popu-
lation at the district level of Serbia.
In  this section, we have present long-term scenario-wise population
projections in disaggregation by sex and age at the level of the district
addressing different demographic perspectives depending on poten-
tially different trajectories of the main components of population
dynamics (fertility, mortality and migration). The level of districts in
Serbia corresponds to NUTS 3, denoted as the level of “small regions
for specific diagnoses” according to the NUTS classification (Eu-
rostat, 2021c). It proved to be the country’s most stable spatial unit in
terms of time variability, which also makes it the lowest spatial level
for producing reliable population forecasts. The projection time hori-
zon covers the long-term period – 2020-2100, similarly to the current
country-level projections of the world and the EU population (UN,
2019a; Eurostat, 2021b). This time horizon approximately matches
the average lifetime span of people in Southern Europe, which allows
a comparison of two almost completely different populations – the
one that consists of ancestors of the projected cohorts, reflecting

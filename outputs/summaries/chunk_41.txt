In a comparative analysis of family policies' effectiveness on fertility trends among OECD countries from 1980 to 2007, Luci-Greulich and Thevenon found that various instruments like paid leave, childcare services, in-cash benefits during children’s age range, and financial transfers postpartum positively impact fertility. They noted a significant shortfall when policies lacked stability due to electoral cycles or unpredictable political landscapes (Luci-Greulich and Thevenon, 2013). A meta-analysis by Stone indicates that offering present value child benefits equivalent to about 10% of household income could increase birth rates between 0.5% and 4.1% (Stone, 2020).

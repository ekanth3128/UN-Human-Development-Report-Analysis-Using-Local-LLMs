Serbia is experiencing high net emigration rates with about 60,000 people leaving annually. Serbia's population trend shows a potential future depopulation due to low fertility and education levels coupled with out-migration since the Balkan conflicts of the early 20th century (16% mortality). As per OECD estimates, over half a million people have left in two decades. Out-migration is driven by economic and cultural factors, leading to Serbia's poor "Talent Retention Index." Without intervention, EU accession may worsen migration trends. Policy support for families and measures against depopulation are urgently needed (Judah, 2019; OECD, 2020).

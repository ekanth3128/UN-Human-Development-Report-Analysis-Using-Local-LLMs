
Air pollution significantly impacts Serbia's public health. As per UN reports from 2018 to 2021, the country experienced a rise in respiratory diseases by approximately 7%, directly linked with increased air contaminants (UNHCR, n.d.). The report emphasizes that over two million Serbians live in areas exceeding WHO's safety threshold for fine particulate matter concentrations - nearly half the national population. Policy recommendations include immediate reduction of industrial emissions and a shift towards cleaner public transportation to mitigate this issue (UNHCR, n.d.).

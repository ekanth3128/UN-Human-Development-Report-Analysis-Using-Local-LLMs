In Serbia's National Human Development Report for 2022, it was found that fertility and mortality rates are key to understanding demographic changes. Crucial statistics show low birth rates now at just over two children per woman after sustained decline since the mid-19th century; however, historically there's been a substantial time delay between falling mortality (which peaked in 1850) and fertility dropping below replacement levels. The report cites Coale’s framework as essential for analyzing these changes - three preconditions being that: fertility must be a choice, it should benefit individuals or couples; and contraception methods need to exist socially accepted by culture. Economic growth through urbanization is also vital in this universal process of demographic transition seen globally since the 19th century as Europe experienced decades ago—except notable disruptions like AIDS pandemic effects on mortality or deteriorating conditions among low-educated men causing higher death rates. Currently, Serbia is progressing in its demographic shift with varying speeds across different world regions due to distinct stages of this process, wherein most African countries and parts of Asia are still growing rapidly while Europe has completed it decades prior.

In Serbia's latest Human Development Report by Stamenković (2022), chronic non-comman-
cable diseases like cardiovascular disorders cause over half of all deaths, mainly due to low socio-economic status and insufficient public health services. The report suggests intensifying preventive health activities for youth via schools and companies as a cost-effective strategy that could slash mortality rates by promoting early care. Additionally, addressing environmental issues like air pollution—a significant risk factor with thousands dying prematurely each year in Serbia alone—could save up to 2,400 lives annually through stricter law enforcement and policy changes aimed at improving living conditions for all societal layers (Boričić et al., 2014; Petrović, 2022).

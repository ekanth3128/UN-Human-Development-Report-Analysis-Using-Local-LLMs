In this report section from the UN Human Development Report for Serbia (2022), it's highlighted that out-migration is particularly high among both low and highly educated individuals, with data indicating over half of all emigrants possessing higher education. This trend could harm human capital development due to their economic interdependence in host countries like Germany, which often prioritize labor market needs (WIC, 2019). Since the Yugoslav Wars and subsequent conflicts, a significant number have moved abroad for menial jobs; however, today's emigrants tend to be more educated. Surveys show that nearly one-quarter of Serbian respondents desire to leave due to economic reasons while others cite low wages (27%), job prospects in their field (21%), and living standards (29%) as motivations for emigration, despite general material improvements over recent decades. Government studies affirm these findings with a call for strategies by the EU to counteract this trend of unintended migration flows during engagement efforts with Western Balkans nations.


In the UN Human Development Report on Serbia's demographics, projections indicate a population decline of 1.4 million or one-fifth by mid-century (Nikitović, 2022). The most affected areas are Belgrade (-3.8%), Vojvodina (-19.4%), Šumadija and Western Serbia (-28.5%), Southern and Eastern Serbia (-33.4%), with Southeastern Serbia experiencing over 40%. To counteract this, demographers suggest prioritizing education to strengthen human resources (Lutz & Gailey, 2020). The report emphasizes the need for balanced regional development and migration management as strategies against depopulation. It also highlights potential resistance towards immigration due to cultural differences (Nikitović, 2019).

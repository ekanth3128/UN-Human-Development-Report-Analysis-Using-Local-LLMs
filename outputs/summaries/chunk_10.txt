In the latest UN Human Development Report for Serbia, demographic decline has been identified over a thirty-year period with statistics showing an estimated population reduction of approximately 315,000 individuals from end 2011 to 2020. To tackle this trend, policies such as generous parental benefits have encouraged births and facilitated home purchasing for young parents through housing incentives aimed at reversing the decline by supporting family life (UNDESA). Concurrently, Serbia grapples with labor shortages due to migration flows into EU member states since 2020. The report suggests that demographic issues extend beyond familial decisions and are entwined with broader socioeconomic factors such as employment policies, education, urban development strategies (UNDESA). To effectively manage these complex dynamics, the Serbian government is urged to expand its focus from personal choices into societal structures that influence demographic trends. Key recommendations include enhancing labor market flexibility and integration measures for immigrants as part of a comprehensive response aimed at mitigating depopulation challenges (UNDESA).

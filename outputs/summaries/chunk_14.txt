Geographical location is a determinant in regional depopulation rates across towns situated near major roads compared to more isolated areas with poorer infrastructure (Antonić, 2022). The construction of new road networks has shown positive effects on local economies and social life. Initiatives like 'studentification' around universities stimulate city growth by fostering higher functions such as services and education sectors in addition to demographic revitalization—as evidenced in Kosovska Mitrovica and Novi Pazar, which witnessed better population figures post-university establishment.

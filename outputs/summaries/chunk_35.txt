In this section of the UN Human Development Report, it is observed that Croatia, Hungary, Slovenia, Romania and Bulgaria will experience significant demographic shifts between now and 2060. In a scenario where these countries emulate Sweden'in high labour participation rates and education levels by 2055, they could see an overall economic burden decline from the ageing population trend despite their current lower fertility rates leading to depopulation. Specifically, productivity-weighted dependency ratios would reduce in Hungary and Romania up to 30%, while Bulgaria experiences a negligible change of around 2%. Slovenia's ratio remains almost constant due to its already high labor participation rate among women (89%) compared to the baseline. The conventional age dependency ratio portrays over an 80% increase in Croatia and Slovenia, but nuanced metrics suggest this figure is less alarming when considering labour force dynamics or potential increases with improvements on these fronts – illustrating that strategies encouraging labor participation may mitigate the economic impact of demographic ageing.

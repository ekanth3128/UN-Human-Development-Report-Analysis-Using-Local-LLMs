In Germany's East and West post-WWII baby boom era (1945–70), fertility rates soared due to a combination of early marriages and economic optimism. Subsequently, both regions witnessed sharp declines in the '70s; however, Eastern Germany implemented strong pro-natal measures like guaranteed housing for pregnant women, financial aid, and childcare facilities which revitalized fertility rates there. Contrastingly, Western German policies focused on softer strategies to balance work with family life without yielding a similar impact (UN report). Between 1976–2015, the number of countries adopting explicit pro-natalist actions globally rose from 10% in '76 to an unprecedented high of 28%, employing strategies such as direct campaigns and parental leave regulations (UN report).

The Serbian population is projected to continue declining in size due to low fertility rates, despite various birth-incentivising strategies adopted since the early 2000s. The government has repeatedly addressed demographic concerns by appointing officials focused on these issues and establishing a dedicated ministry for family care and demography as of recent years. Given Serbia's integration into the EU labour market, there is an impetus to manage migration policy effectively amidst population decline. Policy recommendations include developing new immigration policies alongside traditional pro-natalist measures given that such efforts alone may not be sufficient to reverse or even halt depopulation trends (UNDP).

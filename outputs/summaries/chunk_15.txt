In Serbia, women spend an average of four and a half hours per day on unpaid household chores compared to two for men. Men with higher education who live in urban areas engage more in family caretaking roles (Babović & Stanojević, 2021). Despite high societal values placed upon the family unit, policies are often insensitive towards families and children's needs due to a work culture that struggles with integrating employment-family balance. High youth unemployment rates persist in Serbia (45%) affecting young parents more significantly than those without children. Women face low activity rates and precarious job conditions, primarily attributed to difficulties maintaining both professional and family responsibilities; educated women fare slightly better but still lag behind men’s labor participation by 18% (Tomanović et al., 2016). Initiatives are needed for more work-family balance policies that cater specifically to local contexts.

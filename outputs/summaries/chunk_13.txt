The UN Human Development Report for Serbia highlights significant regional disparities with Belgrade and Novi Sad ranking above national average while other regions lag behind. The HDI score is at a global rank of 64th positioning it among countries like Poland, North Africa, the Caribbean, and East Asia (Nikitović, 2022). These inequalities drive internal migration leading to depopulation particularly affecting medium-sized towns. The Report urges for population policies that consider local particularities of regions suffering from decline while emphasizing on sustainable development strategies across all types of cities (Antonić, 2022).

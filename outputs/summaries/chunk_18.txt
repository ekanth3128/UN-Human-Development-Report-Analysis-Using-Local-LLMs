In Serbia's dual labour market (Aleksić et al., 2021), high income disparities exist between quality jobs in the public sector/large private firms requiring higher education versus lower-quality roles often found within small private businesses. Higher educated workers see their salaries rise, exacerbating this divide and deterring full societal integration (Vuković, 2017). The social assistance reform limits aid to the poorest families of four with income one-fifth above the minimum wage in Serbia, potentially driving migration as individuals seek better opportunities abroad. Youth employment is particularly concerning; those from lower socioeconomic backgrounds or foreign migrants often receive substandard first job offers leading to informal work (SIPRU, 2021). Policies for inclusive economic and labour market integration are suggested as solutions for fostering youth inclusion.

In the UN Human Development Report for Serbia in 2022, key issues are discussed on demographic changes impacting human development with a focus on aging. The report highlights an increasing number of older people reliant on pensions and social care systems amidst low life expectancy (16 years below EU average) at age 65 for men in Serbia; less than ten healthy years are expected once retired, as per WHO data from 2019. Policy recommendations include researching parametric changes like altering the pension indexation formula and adjusting retirement ages with extended life expectancy to counteract systemic pressures without major reforms in the pension framework itself (Matković, 2022). The report underscses gender disparities among those over age 75 who are significantly poorer. It proposes state-subsidized pensions either through a guaranteed minimum income or as an additional module within existing cash benefits schemes for the elderly (Matković, 2021). Serbia's demographic shift necessitates societal and policy adaptations across various sectors to respond effectively.

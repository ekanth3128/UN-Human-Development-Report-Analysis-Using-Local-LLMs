The UN Human Development Report highlighted that Serbia's population decline mirrors historical trends seen during crises like Europe's Great Famine, which reduced its numbers from 6.5 million to 2.8 million between 1841 and 1961 due to high mortality rates amidst food shortages, diseases such as bubonic plague (35M-200M deaths), smallpox, cholera outbreaks, influenza pandemics with an estimated 40M-50M fatalities between 1918 and 1970, HIV/AIDS affecting 25M to 35M individuals since 2002, SARS (200K deaths), MERS (>850K+ cases) from 2014-2016 and the recent COVID-19 pandemic with about 1 million fatalities as of September 28th, 2020. The report suggests that high mortality rates are often mitigated by a historical emphasis on population growth in many societies due to socio-cultural values around procreation and political power but advocates for modern policy approaches focusing on sustainable development amidst demographic changes without endorsing any opinions.

The UN Human Development Report indicates a predicted decline in the total fertility rate (TFR) across Serbia's districts from its current level by up to 21% until 2050, with subregional variations. Districts in South and East show significant reductions of over one-third while Vojvodina experiences a decrease just below this national average at around 19.4%. The baseline scenario forecasts Serbia's population to reduce by about 2 million (from 6.82 million) with an increase towards net immigration and higher fertility rates expected thereafter, ultimately shrinking to roughly 4.14 million in 2100. District-level targets are set according to their historical TFR patterns: aiming for a minimum of 1.9 by mid-century across the country but up to 2.2 targeted towards regions with traditionally lower fertility levels, as indicated through diffusion theory and regional development studies on European contexts (Fox et al., 2019). The likelihood of these high TFR increases shows low probabilities for districts currently below average in fertility but slightly higher chances within the historically more fecund regions.

In Serbia's "medium assumptions" demographic projection, by 2050 the population is expected to decrease from its current size. This decline results primarily due to low fertility rates (1.6 children per woman) and constant migration patterns observed over recent decades being maintained in future projections. Despite these trends leading to a significant ageing of Serbia's populace, whereby the percentage is set to increase from 21% above age 65 today to nearly one-third by mid-century (31%), there are policies that can potentially mitigate dependency issues and foster continued workforce participation. Additionally, educational attainment within Serbia's adult population has been on the rise; however, a potential setback due to COVID-19 could slow but not entirely halt this progress in skill development over time.

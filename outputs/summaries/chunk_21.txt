
In Serbia's Human Development Report (2022), a stark contrast is highlighted between Roma communities from unhygienic settlements, where life expectancy falls short by nearly 13 years compared to the general populace. The report underscores demographic shifts as pivotal in policy-making and calls for multisectoral development policies prioritizing human capital quality over mere population size increases. Despite strides towards health betterment, high mortality rates linked with lifestyle choices remain a hurdle to improving life expectancy within disadvantaged groups. The report suggests that demographic trends should be integrated into all public policy areas and local institutions must become responsive to these issues for balanced development amidst an inevitable shrinking populace (UNHDR, 2022).

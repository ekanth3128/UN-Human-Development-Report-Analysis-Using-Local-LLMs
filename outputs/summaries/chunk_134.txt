In the National Human Development Report for Serbia (2022), it is found that adult education engagement significantly lags behind EU benchmarks, with only a mere 4.3% of adults aged between 25 and 64 participating in learning activities within the last year as per Eurostat data from April 2021, falling short of both Serbia's own strategic targets for education set at 7%. Despite several initiatives such as functional basic education provided by public schools with vocational focuses—serving to provide initial and further qualifications through VET institutions acting similarly in adult educational realms. Also present are nonformal programs aimed towards business development, entrepreneurship, local-level regional growth support via the National Agency for Regional Development (NARD), alongside professional enhancement schemes led by PROAEA organizations which include diverse bodies like companies and associations facilitating continuing education through various modes. However, a systematic approach to monitor adult participation in these programs is absent within Serbian policies. Adult learning initiatives have been sporadically funded with grants from REF (2005) boosting Roma communities before later gaining broader support and scaling up nationally post-IPA's involvement starting 2008; these centers were established to offer tailored adult education programs. Yet, specialized training or liberal arts courses remain notably underdeveloped sectors within the Serbian lifelsoft learning infrastructure (EC, 2021).

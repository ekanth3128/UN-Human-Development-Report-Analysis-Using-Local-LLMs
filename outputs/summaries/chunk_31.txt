In Serbia's projections for demographic change by 2075 under various assumptions—including fertility and mortality trends but without international migration—the population is expected to decline due to the combined effect of age structures from past trends, rising life expectancies contributing positively, alongside stable current fertility rates leading to long-term decrease. Contrastingly, in scenarios with no future migration, EU countries will mostly see a modest growth except for France, Ireland, Sweden, and Finland; others like Iceland, Norway, UK are projected without change or decline while non-EU European nations mirror this trend of natural shrinking. Serbia could experience near 1 million person difference in population by mid-century depending on migration patterns continuation (Luxembourg: 0.57 to potentially decrease; Montenegro, Netherlands, North Macedonia decline significantly under all scenarios).

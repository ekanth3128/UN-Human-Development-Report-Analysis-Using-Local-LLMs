In Serbia's National Human Development Report of 2022, significant challenges due to emigration are highlighted. Skilled individuals aged 30-64 constitute a critical portion (18%) of those leaving the country annually – indicating 'brain drain'. The report suggests strengthening local employment prospects and enhancing regional development as policies for retaining talent, while also emphasizing investments in healthcare to address infertility issues. Education is seen pivotal; particularly preschool education offers broad societal benefits despite underperformance due to depopulation – it's recommended that Serbia increases children’s enrollment across all educational levels and invests further in digital learning infrastructures, teaching force quality, finance allocation towards education, integrity of the system, and lifelong learning opportunities.
